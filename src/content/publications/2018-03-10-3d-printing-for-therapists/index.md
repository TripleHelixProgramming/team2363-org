Triple Helix presented at the [Virginia Occupational Therapy Association's](http://vaota.org) 2018 [School System Symposium](https://vota.wildapricot.org/sss).  The focus of our presentation was how therapy professionals can use skills and tools of "makers," including 3d printing, to make customized products for interventions with their patients.  Our partner in this presentation was Maryland FTC team [Green Machine Reloaded](http://greenmachinereloaded.weebly.com/); this team presented on [their work with the Go Baby Go program](http://greenmachinereloaded.weebly.com/gobabygo.html).

Abstract: *"Makers" use the engineering problem solving process, and their hands, to bring their ideas to life. The competitive high school FIRST robotics teams Triple Helix Robotics and Green Machine Reloaded share the makerspace techniques to rapidly design and create assistive devices. The two teams will demonstrate the tools and technical skills used to 3D print small plastic objects as well as how to modify ride-on toy cars to create low-cost adapted mobility equipment.*
